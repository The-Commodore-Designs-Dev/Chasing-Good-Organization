Meet The Winners

After our extensive search to find good works in New Jersey, the votes are in and these amazing stories took home the top three awards!

First Place, Joann Mehalick of Clark

First place, winning, $1000 is Joann Mehalick of Clark, nominated by her friend Bonnie Busler. Bonnie explains: that years ago, there were no local resources for her friend or her family, to take advantage of at that time to help with mental illness or to learn how the family could adjust. Joann discovered the existence of NAMI (National Alliance on Mental Illness; www.nami.org) There were no local chapters available. My friend and her family felt hopeless. Putting apprehension and fear aside, she dove in headfirst and created the current and only Union County chapter of NAMI and never looked back. In doing so, she was able to help hundreds of families navigate the much misunderstood mental illness dilemma for those with these diseases as well as their bewildered families. She initiated training programs and provided available resources. She initiated the family to family course in the county and taught it for 5 years while providing instruction and materials for new volunteers to teach the program. She introduced police training education to deal with the mentally ill to help alleviate major confrontations and confusion for the mentally ill. She sought out and arranged for professionals to speak to families and those with mental illness. These programs are still being implemented throughout Union County. She has done this all on a volunteer basis for 30+ years! Joann’s determination and passion has been an inspiration to all who know her. Congratulations to Joann!

Second Place, Mobile Meals of Westfield

Second place went to an organization- Mobile Meals of Westfield. They took home $750. Here is some information on the wonderful work they do. *They are a non-profit organization which provides nutritious and delicious prepared meals for those unable to provide meals for themselves. *Their mission is to serve our neighbors in need by preparing/delivering meals & providing caring contact. Mobile Meals of Westfield is a voluntary, 501(c)3 non-profit program, and is available to residents of Clark, Cranford, Fanwood, Garwood, Mountainside, Scotch Plains, and Westfield New Jersey. *They have provided meals for those unable to prepare meals for themselves since 1968. *In their first 50 years of operation, they have served over one million meals to the ill, elderly, injured and disabled living in their seven service communities. *They are made up of a volunteer staff of 150+ people, and a paid staff of only three (a cook, a kitchen assistant and a director of operations). *For more information, visit the Mobile Meals website at www.mobilemealsofwestfield.org or call 908-233-6146. Morgan writes, “I am a volunteer driver and now the volunteer coordinator for Mobile Meals of Westfield. This organization has had a profound impact on my ability to give back to my community. The genuine appreciation and gratitude our clients have for the services we provide, in not only meals but also in conversation and daily contact is invaluable. I think Mobile Meals is a good candidate for this award because of the widespread impact this has on our communities that we serve.” Congratulations to this organization!

Third Place, Andre Williams of Newark

Andre Williams of Newark was nominated by his friend Keith Short. They worked together at UPS for years. Keith writes about his good friend's dedication to helping anyone in need. As a retired UPS worker for over 25 years he's continued to spend his time collecting clothes, coats or by filling up jars with change and giving to homeless people throughout the city. He has a willingness to go beyond for anyone in need on a daily basis. Andre also shared with Chasing Good that he wanted to teach his son valuable life lessons about those in need when he was a young boy. To this day, he keeps pairs of shoes in his car just in case he should see someone in need. For these reasons, he captured our third place award for $500!

Honorable Mention

This year Chasing Good also awarded three worthy recipients with an Honorable Mention for the great things they have done.

First Honorable Mention, Laurie DiRusso of Cranford

Our first Honorable Mention goes to Laurie DiRusso of Cranford, nominated by Jessica Handsman also of Cranford. She was nominated for her extensive volunteer work fostering dogs, connecting them with forever families and being a great resource for all things dog related. She is an authentic person who is dedicated to her causes. She is also a good neighbor and friend. Congratulation to Laurie!

Second Honorable Mention, Pauline Papapietro of Cranford

The next Honorable Mention goes to Pauline Papapietro of Cranford. Rachel Schwartz of Cranford nominated her and writes, “I have MS which has severely impacted my mobility making walking very difficult. This past fall, our house flooded during Hurricane Ida. In addition to the damage to our home, we lost our cars and my mobility equipment. Losing my scooters was a big problem because I could not get around. When Pauline found out I lost my scooters in the flood, she was on a mission to get me a new scooter. Pauline organized and coordinated with a group of wonderful friends to buy me a new one. On September 8th, one week after losing my scooters, my friends surprised me with a new scooter. This was truly amazing. That moment, with this new scooter, my friends gave me a little independence back.”

Third Honorable Mention, Liz DiLuccio and Lucy Santiago

And finally, Liz DiLuccio and Lucy Santiago took home our last Honorable Mention award. Kathleen Cirillo of Cranford nominated this mother and daughter duo for their volunteer work at the Cranford Public Library. She explains, “Liz DiLuccio and her mother, Lucy Santiago, first started volunteering for the book sales run by The Friends of the Cranford Public Library after attending the June 2021 Children’s Sale. In a short time one or the other could be seen sorting and shelving books almost every day the Cranford Library was open. In the weeks before the book sale both Liz and Lucy sorted and shelved books for many hours each day, as more and more books were donated. They expanded the shelving, created appropriate signs, and even added new categories. Liz and Lucy were unfailingly cheerful and energetic during those weeks of preparation. For all that they did, I nominate them for the Chasing Good award.”
